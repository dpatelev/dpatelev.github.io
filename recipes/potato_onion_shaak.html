Potato and Onion Shaak

Shaak is a Gujarati term that means "cooked vegetables", and there are a variety of shaaks in Gujarati cuisine. Other terms exist in Hindi, like sabzi / sabji.

This potato and onion shaak is mainly used as a filling for dosa, a South Indian dish, but it can also be eaten with rotis, naan, daal and rice.

Equipment

  • Non-stick wok / pan

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Onions - red preferable, but either will do!
  • Salt
  • Turmeric
  • Mustard seeds
  • Fenugreek seeds
  • Curry leaves
  • Cumin & coriander powder
  • Cooking oil to temper spices

Steps

  1. Heat cooking oil in non-stick wok / pan to medium high.
  2. Add mustard seeds to oil. When they pop, add the fenugreek seeds and curry leaves, and let them sizzle.
  3. Add potatoes, salt & turmeric, and turn heat down to medium.
  4. Cook potatoes until they are 75% cooked.
  5. Add onions and cumin / coriander powder.
  6. Cook until onions and potatoes are fully cooked.
